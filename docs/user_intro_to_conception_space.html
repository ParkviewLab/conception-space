conception-space

A place to think with the notes you already have.

You don't need another notes app. You need somewhere to think with the notes you've got.

If you use Obsidian, Joplin, Notion, or just a folder of Markdown, you already have the hard part: a corpus of your own ideas. What you don't have is a way to see its shape. conception-space turns your knowledge into a place: a 3D space you build by hand, where every point is one of your real notes, and where you put it is what it means.

Download for macOS · Windows · Linux

What it does

01

Made of real notes, not a new format.

Every node is a handle onto a real file — a Markdown note or a PDF. Open one and you're reading and editing the actual note, in the plain, open formats you already write in. Its points are your notes, arranged so you can fly through them and think.

02

You place it by hand — and the placing is the thinking.

This is the opposite of an auto-generated graph. No layout algorithm decides where things go; you do. Putting this note near that one and far from those is a claim: these belong together; that one doesn't. By the time the space looks right, you've thought the relationships through. The arrangement isn't a picture of your understanding — it is your understanding, made of where things sit.

03

See the structure. Don't reconstruct it.

A list makes you read relationships one at a time and rebuild the whole shape in your head. A space hands the whole shape to your eyes at once: how dense a region is, which connections are heavy, what clusters and what stands alone. A far higher-bandwidth way to grasp how a hundred ideas relate.

04

A place you can come back to.

Because you placed everything, it's spatially memorable, and it lasts across sessions. Leave it, return months later, and the placement itself reminds you what you were thinking when you put that there. A workshop you keep, not a chart you screenshot.

05

Discover what you didn't know was there.

Arrange enough, and structure you never typed begins to show: clusters you didn't plan, gaps where a connection is missing, bridges between fields you thought were separate. The eye catches the pattern before you can put it into words — and that noticing is often the insight you were missing.

The honest specifics

  • Free and open source (AGPL-3.0). Your knowledge, your tool.
  • macOS, Windows, and Linux. The macOS build is Apple-signed and notarized, so it opens without a fight.
  • Plain, open formats — your notes are Markdown and PDF, the same kinds you already write.
  • A real-time 3D space (built on Three.js), not a static diagram: fly through it, save viewpoints, return to them.
  • Early, and moving quickly. The spatial-thinking core is here today. Richer point-and-place authoring, and a room-scale version you can walk through on Apple Vision Pro, are on the way. And exactly how it meets the notes you already keep — its own library, an Obsidian vault, a Joplin server — is an open design question we're working through now.

Try it

Download conception-space and open the bundled example space to feel what a thought-place is like. Then start shaping one around the notes that actually matter to you. Bring the ideas you already have — and begin to see them.
