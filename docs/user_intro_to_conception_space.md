# conception-space — a place to think *with* the notes you already have

**You don't need another notes app. You need somewhere to think with the notes you've got.**

If you use Obsidian, Joplin, Notion, or just a folder of Markdown, you already have the
hard part: a corpus of your own ideas. What you don't have is a way to *see its shape*.
Folders flatten it. Search finds a note but not its neighbourhood. The graph view is a
pretty hairball you glance at and close. The relationships between your ideas live only in
your head — and heads forget.

conception-space is a desktop app that turns your knowledge into a **place**: a 3D space
you build by hand, where every point is one of your real notes, and **where you put it is
what it means**.

---

## Made of real notes, not a new format.

Every node in the space is a **handle onto a real file** — a Markdown note or a PDF. Open a
node and you are reading and editing the actual note, in the plain, open formats you already
write in. The space isn't a diagram *about* your notes; its points *are* your notes, arranged
so you can fly through them and think.

## You place it by hand — and the placing is the thinking.

This is the opposite of an auto-generated graph. No layout algorithm decides where things
go; **you** do. Putting this note near that one and far from those is a claim: *these
belong together; that one doesn't.* By the time the space looks right, you have thought the
relationships through. The arrangement isn't a picture of your understanding — it **is**
your understanding, made of where things sit.

## See the structure. Don't reconstruct it.

A list makes you read relationships one at a time and rebuild the whole shape in your head.
A space hands the whole shape to your eyes at once: how dense a region is, which
connections are heavy, what clusters and what stands alone. Your visual system was built for
exactly this. It is a far higher-bandwidth way to grasp how a hundred ideas relate than any
list can ever be.

## A place you can come back to.

Because *you* placed everything, it is spatially memorable — "the dense cluster, upper
left, is my thesis material." And it lasts across sessions: leave it, return months later,
and the placement itself reminds you what you were thinking when you put that there. It is a
workshop you keep, not a chart you screenshot.

## Discover what you didn't know was there.

Arrange enough, and structure you never typed begins to show: clusters you didn't plan, gaps
where a connection is missing, bridges between fields you thought were separate. The eye
catches the pattern before you can put it into words — and that noticing is often the
insight you were missing.

---

## How it differs from the tools you already use

|  | Obsidian / Joplin / Notion | conception-space |
|---|---|---|
| **Core job** | store, link, and search your notes | give you a *place to think* with them |
| **The graph** | auto-laid-out, decorative, glanced at | hand-built, meaningful, inhabited |
| **Where meaning lives** | in the text and the links | *also* in where things sit |
| **The material** | text notes and links | real Markdown & PDF notes, placed in 3D space |

It is not a replacement for your note app. It is the spatial place to think that those tools
don't give you.

## The honest specifics

- **Free and open source** (AGPL-3.0). Your knowledge, your tool.
- **macOS, Windows, and Linux.** The macOS build is Apple-signed and notarized, so it opens
  without a fight.
- **Plain, open formats** — your notes are Markdown and PDF, the same kinds you already write.
- **A real-time 3D space** (built on Three.js), not a static diagram: fly through it, save
  viewpoints, and return to them.
- **Early, and moving quickly.** The spatial-thinking core is here today. Richer
  point-and-place authoring and a room-scale version you can walk *through* (on Apple Vision
  Pro) are on the way. And exactly how it meets the notes you already keep — its own library,
  an Obsidian vault, a Joplin server — is an open design question we are working through now.

## Try it

[Download the latest release](https://github.com/ParkviewLab/conception-space/releases) for
your platform and open the bundled example space to feel what a thought-place is like. Then
start shaping one around the notes that actually matter to you. Bring the ideas you already
have — and begin to *see* them.
